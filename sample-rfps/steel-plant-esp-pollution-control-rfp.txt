REQUEST FOR PROPOSAL (RFP)
Supply, Installation, and Commissioning of Electrostatic Precipitator (ESP) for Sinter Plant - Waste Gas Cleaning System

Issued By: JSW Steel Limited - Vijayanagar Works
RFP Reference: JSW/VNG/ESP/2026/047
Issue Date: March 15, 2026
Submission Deadline: May 30, 2026

1. INTRODUCTION
JSW Steel Limited, India's leading steel manufacturer, invites proposals from qualified Original Equipment Manufacturers (OEMs) and EPC contractors for the design, engineering, supply, installation, commissioning, and performance guarantee of a Head End Electrostatic Precipitator (ESP) for the Sinter Plant waste gas cleaning system at its Vijayanagar Integrated Steel Works, Bellary, Karnataka.

This project is mandated under the Ministry of Environment, Forest and Climate Change (MoEFCC) revised emission norms for the iron and steel sector (2024 notification), which require particulate matter emissions below 30 mg/Nm³ from all sinter plant operations by December 2027.

2. PROJECT SCOPE

2.1 Core ESP System
The selected vendor shall be responsible for:
- Design and engineering of Head End ESP for Sinter Plant waste gas cleaning
- Gas Volume Handling Capacity: 13,50,000 m³/hr (minimum)
- Inlet Dust Load: Up to 2 g/Nm³ (with peak loads up to 5 g/Nm³ during upset conditions)
- Guaranteed Outlet Emission: < 30 mg/Nm³ (as per CPCB norms)
- Collection Efficiency: ≥ 98.5%
- ESP Configuration: Minimum 4 fields, weighted wire-plate type
- Operating Temperature Range: 80°C to 200°C
- Gas Composition: SOx, NOx, CO, HCl, HF, heavy metals, dioxins/furans in trace quantities
- Pressure Drop: ≤ 250 Pa across ESP

2.2 Mechanical Scope
- ESP casing with thermal insulation (80mm mineral wool)
- Collecting plates (cold-rolled steel, minimum 1.2mm thickness)
- Discharge electrodes (rigid frame type preferred)
- Rapping mechanism (tumbling hammer for collecting plates, magnetic impulse for discharge electrodes)
- Hoppers with heating arrangements and level indicators
- Ash handling and conveying system (chain conveyor + pneumatic transport)
- Inlet and outlet ductwork modifications (from existing sinter machine to ESP and from ESP to existing ID fans)
- Stack modifications if required
- Support structure and civil foundation design

2.3 Electrical & Instrumentation
- Transformer-Rectifier (TR) sets: High-frequency switch-mode power supply (SMPS) type preferred
- Automatic Voltage Controllers (AVC) with microprocessor-based control
- ESP performance monitoring system (opacity meter, gas flow, temperature, pressure)
- Continuous Emission Monitoring System (CEMS) - CPCB approved
- Integration with existing plant DCS (Siemens PCS7)
- Motor Control Center (MCC) and Power Distribution
- PLC-based ESP control system with HMI

2.4 Civil Works
- Foundation for ESP structure (RCC raft/pile foundation as per soil investigation)
- Equipment foundation for TR sets, control room, ash handling
- Cable trenches and pipe racks
- Control room building (approx. 80 sqm)

2.5 Commissioning & Performance
- Cold and hot commissioning
- Performance Guarantee Test (PGT) as per IS 10000 / equivalent international standard
- 72-hour continuous performance run
- Stack emission testing by NABL accredited laboratory
- Training for plant O&M staff (minimum 40 hours)

3. PLANT DETAILS & OPERATING CONDITIONS

3.1 Sinter Plant Specifications
- Sinter Machine: 312 m² effective sintering area
- Production Capacity: 5.5 MTPA sinter
- Operating Hours: 8,000 hours/year (continuous operation)
- Existing Pollution Control: Multi-cyclone (primary) - to be retained as pre-collector
- Existing ID Fan Capacity: 2 x 7,500 kW (sufficient for additional ESP pressure drop)

3.2 Site Conditions
- Location: Vijayanagar Works, Toranagallu, Bellary District, Karnataka
- Ambient Temperature: 15°C to 45°C
- Relative Humidity: 20% to 95%
- Seismic Zone: III (IS 1893)
- Rainfall: 600 mm/year
- Wind Speed: 150 km/h (design)
- Soil Bearing Capacity: 15 T/m² (preliminary - detailed soil investigation by vendor)

3.3 Utility Availability
- Power Supply: 33 kV (vendor to provide step-down transformers)
- Instrument Air: 6 bar, dry and oil-free
- Cooling Water: Available from plant circuit
- Compressed Air: 7 bar at ESP battery limit
